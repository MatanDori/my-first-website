Opening

Amphibians are vertebrants that in their yougth live in a fresh water and in adulthood are able to exist outside of water as well. The first amphibians evolved as the devonian from fish with fleshy fins that had lungs and bony fins, features that were useful for adapting to life on land. Over time they split and diversified. They became dominant in the Carboniferous and permian periods, but were eventually replaced by reptiles and other wertebrates. At the same time, their size bacame smaller and diversity decreased, until finally only one subclass survive: extant amphibians.

Evolution

Salamanders Yes Yes

Their bodies are elongated and thin, their legs are short and equal, and their tails are long. Due to their moist skin, their maximum suitability is for habitats close to water reservoirs, or in habitats where the humidity is relatively high. Some of the species live mainly in an aquatic environment, some enter and leave the water alternately, and the other part live on land throughout their entire lives as adults.

Fire salamander, banded newt, Near Eastern fire salamander, Pleurodelinae
Frogs and Toads No Yes

The characteristics of adult tailless are long hind legs, a short, broad and relatively low body, webbed fingers stretched between them, prominent and relatively large eyes and the absence of a tail. Most of the tailless live a semi-aquatic lifestyle, but they get around easily on land by diving or climbing.

Levant water frog, Hyla savignyi, European green toad
